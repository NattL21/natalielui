The Problem

Current sanitary product dispensers, specifically observed within educational institutions, are inadequate, often found understocked or broken. While many bathrooms are equipped with feminine hygiene product dispensers, more often than not they were not utilized to their full potential. We found that this left many people unable to access products when they needed them.

Our Product

Our team strived to answer the question of how we could increase ACCESSIBILITY to feminine hygiene products while maintaining EFFICIENCY and COST CONSCIENTIOUSNESS.

S.P.E.N.C.E.R., is a dispensing machine that provides on-hand sanitary products, utilizing advanced sensor technology to determine product inventory as well as an app that provides easy access to track information in regards to each machine.

User Research

In order to properly assess how to improve access to sanitary products, we researched the current state of sanitary dispensation options and their shortcomings.

A tap to pay option on top of the classic coin payment option in order to modernize payment collection - while the ultimate goal is to be able to provide free access to hygiene products, we understand that many organizations may not be able to afford this. Therefore, we hoped to make our machines as financially accessible as possible. An internal tracker created using raspberry pi magnetic switches. One switch is a bar that rests on the top of the product chute, moving down as products are dispensed. Once the switches are close enough in vicinity, the mechanism sends a notification to the app that stock is low in that particular machine.

An app prioritizing efficiency and clarity that allows users to track all the machines linked to the same account. The app includes details and updated stock levels of each machine, presented in both a list and map view - thus allowing users to visually see where all their machines are and track the statistics of each individual machine.

Impact and Expansion

Our product successfully addresses the issues that women currently have with public dispensers which are: difficulties to pay for products and under-stocked, broken dispensers.

Our user surveys found that 76% responded that they never use the sanitary product dispensers at school, but if they were given a Spencer dispenser, 81% said they would use them.
